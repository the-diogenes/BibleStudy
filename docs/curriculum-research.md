# Curriculum research (for you to choose from)

This is a neutral shortlist to help you pick a study approach. It is intentionally
*not* a recommendation of one plan; you decide what fits your group. Sources lean
toward serious, historically-grounded, conservative study (with respect for the
broader Christian tradition), per your stated preferences.

## 1. Pick a study *method* first

Most groups land on one of these rhythms. They are compatible with any book.

- **Inductive method (Observation -> Interpretation -> Application).** The most
  widely used small-group framework. "What does it say? What does it mean? How do
  I respond?" Strong for men's groups because it keeps discussion anchored to the
  text rather than opinion.
  - Precept Ministries / Kay Arthur (inductive, verse-by-verse, conservative).
  - The Bible Project (free videos) pairs well as a book overview before drilling in.
- **Verse-by-verse exposition (lectio continua).** Work straight through a book,
  passage by passage. Pairs naturally with this app's reader + per-passage threads.
- **Lectio Divina (read, meditate, pray, contemplate).** A historic, devotional
  reading rhythm from the monastic tradition (valued in Orthodox/Catholic practice).
  Good for shorter passages and prayer-focused weeks.

## 2. Pick a *scope* (what to study first)

- **A single Gospel** (John is a common first choice for its theology of Christ;
  Mark for pace; Matthew for the fulfillment-of-promise theme).
- **A Pauline letter** (Romans for doctrine; Ephesians/Philippians for shorter,
  high-impact studies; good for the "saved through Christ alone" emphasis).
- **A whole-Bible arc** (creation -> covenant -> Christ -> church -> consummation),
  to see how the covenant with Abraham is fulfilled in Christ.
- **Topical, but text-driven** (e.g., manhood/discipleship, prayer, the Law and
  grace) - anchor each session in specific passages so it stays expository.

## 3. Reputable sources and tools

Free / built into this app:

- **Free Use Bible API** (already integrated) - Berean Standard Bible, WEB, KJV, etc.
- **Public-domain commentaries** (in the app's Passage view): Jamieson-Fausset-Brown,
  Matthew Henry, Keil & Delitzsch (OT), John Gill, Adam Clarke. Traditional and conservative.
- **Original languages** (in the app): Greek/Hebrew with Strong's + morphology.

Worth knowing / often referenced (you'd source separately):

- **Ancient Christian Commentary on Scripture (ACCS)** - the church fathers
  (Athanasius, Chrysostom, Augustine, etc.) verse-by-verse. Excellent if you want
  the seriousness and continuity of the early church.
- **Orthodox Study Bible** and the **Catholic** Navarre/Ignatius Study Bible -
  for the historic, liturgical reading of texts.
- **Wesleyan / Nazarene resources** - matching a Nazarene background:
  - *Wesley Study Bible* / Wesley's *Explanatory Notes upon the New Testament*.
  - The Foundry Publishing (Church of the Nazarene) study materials.
- **Men's-ministry frameworks** for the *group* dynamics (not doctrine):
  - Man in the Mirror (Patrick Morley) - weekly study format and leader tips.
  - Inductive leader guidance from Precept and Cru.

## 4. Practical format that fits this app

A repeatable weekly loop, all supported by the site:

1. Admin adds the week's passage as a **lesson** (Home checklist).
2. Members **read** the passage (switch translations; use interlinear for key words).
3. Members post **observations/questions** in the passage **discussion**.
4. The group meets; capture takeaways as **group notes**; keep personal **private notes**.
5. Mark the lesson **done**; the next becomes **active**.

Group size and rhythm (from men's-ministry sources): 6-12 men, fixed weekly time,
start/end on time, leader talks <25% of the time, every man gets "air time," and
each session ends with one concrete application.

When you've chosen a method + first book, the admin tools can scaffold the whole
plan as lessons in a few minutes.
